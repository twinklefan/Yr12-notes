    Fractions - multiply

  • Multiply top numbers (numerators)
  • Multiply bottom numbers (denominators)
  • Simplify

    • Fractions - divide

  • Flip second fraction upside down
  • Change ÷ to ×
  • Multiply as normal

    • Fractions - add

  • To add fractions together, the denominators need to be the same
  • Multiply one or both fractions until denominators are the same
  • Add the numerators together and keep the common denominator

    • Fractions - subtract

  • To subtract fractions, it is similar to adding as the denominators need to be the same
  • Multiply one or both fractions until denominators are the same
  • Subtract the numerators and keep the common denominator

    • Completing the square

  • For this type of equation: ax²+bx+c=0
  • Divide the equation by a, so ax² becomes x² (if needed)
  • Divide b by 2, then square it: (b÷2)²
  • Add or subtract from the equation, until c is equal to the number calculated above: (number calulated above-c)
  • Factorise
  • Square root
  • Add or subtract left-over number, until x is alone on one side

    • Write in form (x+a)²+b

  • Converting mx²+nx+p=0 to (x+a)²+b=0
  • Divide n by 2, then square it: (n÷2)²
  • Add or subtract until p is equal to number calculated above
  • Factorise
  • Add or subtract until one side of equation equals 0

    • Factorising awkward equation

  • For ax²+bx+c
  • Multiply end terms (a & c)
  • Find two terms that multiply number calulated above, and add to middle term(b)
  • Replace bx with the new terms found above
  • Factorise
